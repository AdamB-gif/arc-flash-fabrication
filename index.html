Common Questions

REAL ANSWERS

Not "call us for pricing." Straight answers to the questions we hear every week.

Yes — that's our specialty. We start from raw mild steel: C-channel for the main frame, square tube for the decking supports, angle iron for cross-members, and DOM tubing where needed. We cut, fit, and weld to your exact dimensions and load rating. Nothing comes from a kit.

It depends on the repair. A simple weld repair on a coupler or cross-member typically runs $100–$250. Structural frame repairs with gussets and section replacement range $300–$800+. Floor replacements run $400–$1,200 depending on size. We give free same-day estimates so you know the cost before any work begins.

Yes, when done correctly. A proper frame repair means grinding the crack out completely, fitting a gusset plate or sleeve over the repair zone, and making full-penetration welds — not just running a bead over the surface. We assess whether the damage is repairable or if a section needs full replacement and won't sign off on work that isn't structurally sound.

Small repairs (weld, coupler, lights, cross-member) are typically same-day or next day. Larger jobs like full floor replacements or structural rebuilds take 2–5 business days. Custom fabrication builds are quoted with a specific turnaround at the time of estimate — usually 1–3 weeks for a full trailer build depending on complexity.

Mild steel is more affordable and easier to repair — great for work trailers. It needs paint or coating to prevent rust in Florida's humidity. Aluminum is lighter (better fuel economy), won't rust, but costs more and is harder to field-repair. Stainless is best for food-grade or marine applications. For most utility and work trailers in Tampa Bay, mild steel with a good epoxy primer is the right call.

Absolutely. We build custom smokers from scratch — offset, reverse flow, vertical, or trailer-mounted competition rigs. We fabricate the firebox, cooking chamber, tuning plates, exhaust stacks, and doors to your specs. Most builds use 3/16" or 1/4" plate steel for heat retention. Have a sketch? We'll turn it into metal.

Yes — bars, restaurants, and breweries are some of our best custom work. We fabricate stainless steel bar foot rails, bar tops, rail brackets, table frames, shelf supports, wall-mounted fixtures, and custom furniture frames. We work in food-grade stainless where required. Bring us your measurements and we'll quote it out.

Yes. We fabricate custom food trailer and concession trailer frames — tongue, axles, frame rails, floor supports — to your exact dimensions. We build the steel chassis from raw material so you can outfit the interior however you need. Whether it's a small hot dog cart trailer or a full BBQ competition trailer, we can fabricate it. We serve food vendors and small businesses across Pinellas, Hillsborough, and Pasco counties.

Yes — we design and fabricate custom driveway gates, security gates, and ornamental iron fencing from mild steel, square tube, and flat bar. We build to your exact measurements and opening width, and can weld in decorative elements, hinges, and latch hardware. Gates are a finished product we deliver to you. Popular for residential driveways, commercial lots, and fenced yards across Pinellas and Pasco counties.

We use MIG (GMAW) and stick (SMAW) depending on the material and job. MIG is our primary process for trailer fabrication — fast and strong on mild steel. Stick welding is used for heavy repair work and thick plate. We work in mild steel and stainless steel and choose the right process for each application.

Yes. We fabricate custom steel frames for outdoor kitchens, BBQ islands, and backyard cooking setups. We build the steel skeleton — the frame, grill cutouts, countertop supports, and cabinet framing — and you finish with stone, tile, or stucco. We also fabricate stainless steel countertops and flat-bar trim pieces for a clean, commercial-grade finish. If you've got a plan or a sketch, bring it in.
