Yes, when done correctly. A proper frame repair means grinding the crack out completely, fitting a gusset plate or sleeve over the repair zone, and making full-penetration welds — not just running a bead over the surface. We assess whether the damage is repairable or if a section needs full replacement and won't sign off on work that isn't structurally sound.

Small repairs (weld, coupler, lights, cross-member) are typically same-day or next day. Larger jobs like full floor replacements or structural rebuilds take 2–5 business days. Custom fabrication builds are quoted with a specific turnaround at the time of estimate — usually 1–3 weeks for a full trailer build depending on complexity.

Mild steel is more affordable and easier to repair — great for work trailers. It needs paint or coating to prevent rust in Florida's humidity. Aluminum is lighter (better fuel economy), won't rust, but costs more and is harder to field-repair. Stainless is best for food-grade or marine applications. For most utility and work trailers in Tampa Bay, mild steel with a good epoxy primer is the right call.
